History

Whitewater rafting has a rich history that dates back thousands of years. The earliest evidence of rafting can be traced to ancient civilizations such as the Egyptians and Mesopotamians, who used rafts made from reeds and other natural materials to navigate rivers for transportation and trade. In the modern era, whitewater rafting as a recreational activity began to gain popularity in the mid-20th century, particularly in the United States. The sport has since evolved with advancements in equipment and safety measures, making it accessible to a wider audience. Today, whitewater rafting is enjoyed by adventure enthusiasts around the world, offering thrilling experiences on rivers of varying difficulty levels.
